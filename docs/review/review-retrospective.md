# Review Retrospective (Exercise 6.2)

## What worked well?

The direct interaction with a member of the reviewed team was very helpful. It allowed us to clarify open questions quickly and gain insights into design decisions that were not fully documented.  
The live discussion format also made it easier to understand the overall system structure and intentions behind certain implementation choices.

---

## What was difficult?

The review process was affected by several organizational challenges. The repository links were provided late compared to the available time, and the repository itself was private, which prevented proper preparation before the meeting and raised more questions.
As a result, the review had to be conducted in a more ad-hoc and discussion-driven manner rather than following a structured inspection process.

Additionally, only one member of the reviewed team was available during the meeting, which limited the depth of explanations in some areas. The short duration (45 minutes) also constrained the level of detail we could achieve.

## Are formal reviews suitable?

Formal reviews are generally suitable and valuable for our team, especially for:

- Requirements and specifications

- System architecture and design decisions

- Data models

These artefacts benefit the most from structured feedback and early defect detection.

However, formal reviews are less effective when artefacts are incomplete or when access is restricted, as seen in this case. In such situations, a more informal walkthrough was more practical.

## Improvement for the next review

For future reviews, early access to the repository and artefacts should be ensured so that reviewers can properly prepare in advance.
Additionally, preparing a structured checklist before the meeting would improve efficiency and lead to more systematic results and less post-effort.
